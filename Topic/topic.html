    Topic 3: Vietnamese gestures and customs
    Vietnamese gestures and customs hold great significance in the country's cultural identity. When meeting someone for the first time, a warm smile accompanied by a bow or nod is a common way to greet each other. Respect for elders is deeply ingrained, and using specific honorifics and bowing are customary when addressing them. For peers and younger individuals, a friendly nod or a simple "xin chào" suffices. Handshakes are used in formal and business settings, often presented with both hands as a sign of respect. In the business world, exchanging business cards is a polite practice, done with both hands, and cards are read and acknowledged before being put away. Topics for small talk often revolve around family, health, and general well-being, while sensitive subjects are best avoided. To indicate numbers or point at someone, open hands are commonly used. Overall, understanding and embracing these gestures and customs will foster positive interactions and showcase cultural appreciation in Vietnam.
